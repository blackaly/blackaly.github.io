In Defence of Boredom

Monday, 20 November 2023

We have declared war on boredom. Every idle moment must be filled. We carry devices that promise infinite entertainment. We have forgotten how to wait, how to be still, how to be alone with our thoughts.

This is a loss greater than we know.

The Uses of Emptiness

Boredom is not mere absence. It is a space in which something can happen. When the mind is not occupied, it wanders. It makes unexpected connections. It surfaces thoughts long submerged.

Many of our best ideas come in moments of apparent idleness. The shower. The long walk. The sleepless hour before dawn. These are not wasted time. They are the seedbed of creativity.

The Fear of Silence

Why do we fear boredom so? Perhaps because it forces us to confront ourselves. In silence, we cannot escape our own company. The thoughts we have been avoiding rise up to meet us.

This is uncomfortable. But it is also necessary. Self-knowledge requires that we sometimes sit with ourselves, without distraction, without entertainment.

A Modest Proposal

Try this experiment. For one hour, do nothing. No phone. No book. No music. Simply sit and let your mind wander where it will.

The first minutes will be difficult. You will feel restless, anxious. But if you persist, something will shift. The restlessness will give way to calm. Thoughts will arise, unbidden and surprising.

This is what boredom offers, if we let it.
