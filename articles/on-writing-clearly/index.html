On Writing Clearly

Friday, 15 December 2023

Clear writing is clear thinking made visible. When our prose is confused, it is usually because our ideas are confused. The discipline of writing forces us to discover what we actually believe.

The Enemy of Clarity

The chief enemy of clear writing is pretension. We reach for impressive words when simple ones would serve. We construct elaborate sentences to hide the thinness of our thought. We mistake obscurity for depth.

Good writing is honest. It says what it means in the plainest possible terms. It does not show off. It serves the reader, not the writer’s vanity.

Practical Counsel

Some rules are worth following:

  • Prefer short words to long ones
  • Prefer concrete words to abstract ones
  • Prefer active voice to passive
  • Cut every word that does not earn its place
  • Read your work aloud

These are not laws. They can be broken when there is reason. But they are good defaults.

The Test of Time

The prose that lasts is almost always simple. Read the King James Bible. Read Lincoln. Read Orwell. The sentences are short. The words are plain. The meaning is clear.

Fashion in writing comes and goes. Clarity endures.
