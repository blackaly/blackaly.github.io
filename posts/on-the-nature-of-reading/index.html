On the Nature of Reading

Monday, 15 January 2024

Reading is not merely the act of decoding symbols on a page. It is a conversation across time, a meeting of minds separated by centuries yet united in thought.11. really?

When we read the ancients, we do not simply consume their words. We enter into dialogue with them. We question, we agree, we dispute. The text becomes a living thing, breathing new meaning with each encounter.

The Lost Art of Slow Reading

Our age has forgotten how to read slowly.22. Great

We skim, we scan, we extract. But true reading requires patience. It demands that we surrender ourselves to the rhythm of another’s thought.

Consider how the scholars of old approached a text. They read aloud. They memorized. They copied by hand. Each word was savored, each sentence weighed. This was not inefficiency—it was reverence.
