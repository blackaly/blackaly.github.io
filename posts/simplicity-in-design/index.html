Simplicity in Design

Wednesday, 10 January 2024

The pursuit of simplicity is not a retreat from complexity. It is the hard-won result of understanding what is essential and having the courage to remove everything else.

A simple design does not happen by accident. It requires more thought, more refinement, more willingness to discard what does not serve the whole.

The Tradition of Less

The great craftsmen understood this. A Shaker chair. A Japanese tea bowl. A Roman arch. Each achieves its beauty not through addition but through restraint.

  1. I am
  2. Where

We have much to learn from these traditions. In our age of excess, the discipline of simplicity is a radical act.

Against Decoration

Ornament for its own sake is a kind of dishonesty. It distracts from function. It hides poor craftsmanship. It flatters without substance.

True beauty needs no embellishment. It emerges naturally from fitness to purpose, from honest materials, from careful proportion.
